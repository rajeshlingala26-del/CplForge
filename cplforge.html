FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before getting started with us.

What's the minimum ad spend you'll work with?

We typically take on accounts spending ₹3L/month or more across channels. Below that, media math rarely has enough volume to optimise meaningfully — we'll tell you honestly if that's your situation.

How does your pricing compare to just running ads ourselves or hiring in-house?

Our fee is built to pay for itself in the CPL drop alone. Most accounts we take over were losing more to unoptimised spend, missed negatives, and stale creative than our entire retainer costs — and you skip the time and cost of hiring, training, and managing an in-house media buyer.

Do you take a percentage of ad spend?

We offer both a flat monthly retainer and a hybrid model tied to a target CPL or CAC. We'll recommend whichever fits your stage on the audit call.

How fast will we see a change in cost-per-lead?

Most accounts see a measurable shift by week 4–5, once new creative and landing pages have enough impressions to read cleanly. Anything faster is usually a fluke, not a fix.

How many leads can we realistically expect?

It depends on your category, budget, and current funnel — we'll give you a real range on the audit call, not a generic promise. What we can say is most clients see a noticeable uptick in qualified volume by month two or three, once the account has been rebuilt around the right intent signals.

Will we get regular reporting, and how often?

Yes — a monthly reporting call that walks through spend, CPL, and what changed and why, plus a live dashboard in between. We flag what's underperforming as soon as we see it; we don't wait for the monthly call to tell you something's off.

Do you also handle the creative, or just the media buying?

Both. Media without creative testing is just spend; we run in-house design and copy so ad iterations ship in days, not through a separate agency handoff.

What makes CPLForge different from other agencies we've talked to?

Most agencies either over-promise on strategy decks or spread themselves across every channel and specialism. We stay narrow — Google, Meta, and LinkedIn, run by people who live inside cost-per-lead — so we go deeper on the few things that actually move your numbers instead of doing a little of everything.

Is there a minimum commitment period?

We ask for an initial 3-month runway, because paid media needs enough data cycles to optimise properly — anything shorter and you're judging us on a half-finished test. After that, it's month-to-month; we'd rather earn the renewal than lock you into a contract.
